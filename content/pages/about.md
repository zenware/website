## Who Am I?

I'm **Jay** — a software engineer who builds reliable systems at scale, across **data engineering, infrastructure, and security**. I'm a lifelong autodidact and I genuinely cannot keep my paws off the *shiny new thing*, but the throughline of my career is making complex systems dependable: the kind where "downtime" isn't an inconvenience, it's a power plant.

My path went broad before it went deep:

- **2010–2017** — Helped local businesses solve problems with computers; web and software development (and organized/taught freeCodeCamp in central Illinois).
- **2017–2019** — Supported Fortune 500 ISP operations across the US.
- **2019–2021 · Verve Industrial Protection** — Wore every hat while helping build the team and platform: system architecture and domain modeling, reverse-engineering industrial network protocols to build agentless device inventory, maintaining an ICS-aware fork of Ansible, and literally building the physical hardware lab from scratch (rolling 2x4-and-DIN-rail benches, gear sourced and unlocked for testing).
- **2021–2024 · Dragos** — Founding hire, twice. First on the *Knowledge Pack* team, where I took platform releases from "nobody can build this end-to-end" to a CI/CD pipeline producing complete online and offline ISO gold images on demand — with separate content-only and code-only release tracks so detection updates could ship into NERC-CIP environments without touching code. Then on the founding *Data* team, owning all platform data (ELK plus every SQL/NoSQL store and message queue): I built a custom Elastic index-management service, consolidated scattered SQL and MongoDB onto a single PostgreSQL, and built a lightweight backup/restore system for disaster recovery and environment promotion (QA→Staging→Prod, and Prod→Prod replication across plants).
- **2024–present** — Taking intentional time at home for the first couple years of my daughter's life, while staying sharp through reading, open source, and writing here. Now actively returning to work.

The ICS/OT world taught me reliability where the stakes are physical — and the range, from bare-metal hardware to CI/CD to data-platform architecture, travels to any team that cares about systems staying up.

## Why This Blog?

My hope is to finally [Learn in Public](https://www.swyx.io/learn-in-public). The content here is primarily for me, but if you're here I hope you get some use out of it too. I also have the fantasy that I'll one day convert decades of private notes into something fit for public consumption — expect material on Linux, software, web dev, and building/maintaining self-hosted infrastructure.
